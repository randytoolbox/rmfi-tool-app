# RMFI Manufacturing — Humanoid Robot Industry Prospect List
**Prepared:** June 2026  
**Purpose:** Precision machining / contract manufacturing opportunities in the humanoid robot supply chain

---

## WHY THIS SECTOR NOW

Humanoid robots crossed from R&D into production in 2026. Figure AI's factory is producing one robot per hour. Boston Dynamics began shipping its electric Atlas. Tesla is targeting mass production of Optimus. Every one of these robots requires precision-machined joints, actuators, housings, frames, and structural components — the kind of work RMFI does.

The companies building their own machine shops are protecting proprietary core designs. Standard structural parts, enclosures, brackets, and non-proprietary components get outsourced. That is the opening.

## WHAT TO LEAD WITH IN SALES CALLS

1. **Tolerances you can hold** — robot joints require ±0.001" or tighter on mating surfaces
2. **Materials experience** — 6061/7075 aluminum, titanium 6Al-4V, 17-4 stainless are the common robot alloys
3. **Prototype-to-production capability** — these companies move fast; the shop that handles their prototypes often gets the production order
4. **Lead time** — robot companies are scaling quickly and hate supply chain delays
5. **AS9100 / ISO 9001 certification** — defense robotics especially requires certified quality systems
